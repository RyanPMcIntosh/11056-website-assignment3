The Viking Long Ship

Usage and exploration were central to the role of Viking boats in the medieval era. Primarily, these vessels, such as longships and knarrs, served as versatile tools for raiding, trade, and exploration across the vast expanse of the North Sea and beyond. Longships, sleek and swift, were the epitome of Viking naval prowess, designed for lightning-fast strikes along coastal settlements, enabling the Vikings to swiftly navigate shallow rivers and reach far-off lands. On the other hand, knarrs, sturdier and broader, facilitated extensive trade networks, carrying goods and resources to and from distant shores, opening up avenues for commerce and cultural exchange. Both types of vessels played a pivotal role in the Viking Age, enabling the Norse people to leave an indelible mark on history through their maritime exploits and voyages of discovery.
